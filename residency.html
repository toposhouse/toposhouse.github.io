Topos Residency

Topos House was founded on the principles of life time learning. Residencies are organised to gather domain experts from different fields with the purpose of solving a single question, and teaching each other along the way.

​Residents finish by gaining complementary knowledge outside of their starting field allowing for a polymath life approach, as well as having made single contributions to the residency topic.

The Neurolearning Problem 

The Fall 2019 Residency is centered around learning; specifically, developing a model of human learning.

Convolutional Nets, ImageNet and reinforcement learning allowed for an expansive jump in the ways in which machines could "learn", but how? ​

Can insight from human vision and neurological development drive developments of machine learning and can neural nets be created to match human learning.

​Through experiments, lectures, and week day work sessions the Fall Residency aims to provide answers to our 2019 question: ​

How do humans learn?

The Mind of a Learner 

This is not a question that can be answered from one field. We are holding an open call for:

Neuroscientists, Mechanical Engineers, Mathematicians, Data Scientists, Machine Learning Engineers, Education Experts, Cognitive Psychologists, Physicists, Neurobiologists and Computer Scientists.

There are onsite lodgings and part time commitments that are available for all applicants.

​Residents will finish their residency having contributed to written body of work that captures the methodology and results from the residency, as well as having fostered career development from a resident from another domain.

Residency Scope​

The residency follows a 14 week format.

Teams will complete projects and experiments in 2 week cycles.

These will form 6 "Epics" with the final 2 weeks for an integration and consolidation of learning.
